Types of Error

The ABB Fair Process classifies errors, violations or mistakes within the organisation into 4 different types.

Click on each one to learn more.

Human Error
An error caused by the fact people are not 100% reliable all the time. It's only natural and common that some mistakes will be made when human input is involved.
Avoidable Error

An error which could have been avoided if things had been done differetly. For example, an error that was caused by inadequate training, unclear procedures or deficient process.

'Due Care' Violation

An error caused by someone not taking a reasonable amount of care in performing a task. This lack of 'due care' can often be caused by work pressure or the repetitive nature of the task.

Wilful Misconduct

An error caused by someone who chose to take the action they did in the full knowledge of what the consequences would be and fully inteding to achieve that outcome. The performed their actions wilfully.
